# Business Context

*\[Establish the organizational and strategic environment in which the business problem exists, providing sufficient context to understand its origin and relevance before formal problem definition.\]*

*\[Provide a structured overview of the business environment by describing:*

* *where the problem originates within the organization*  
* *how this area relates to strategic priorities*  
* *what the current operational situation looks like at a high level*

*Do not define or interpret the problem yet \- focus on objective context and positioning.\]*

*\[**Example**: The organization operates in a competitive retail banking environment where customer retention is a key driver of long-term profitability.*

*Customer lifecycle management has become a strategic focus area due to increasing market competition and evolving customer expectations.\]*

## Organizational Context 

*\[Describe the organizational unit or functional area where the problem originates and its role within the broader enterprise.\]*

*\[Identify the business unit, its responsibilities, and how it contributes to organizational objectives.*

*Focus on structural and functional positioning rather than performance issues or problems.*

*Avoid describing challenges or inefficiencies (these belong to Section [Business Problem Statement](#business-problem-statement)).\]*

*\[**Example**: The Customer Lifecycle Management Department operates within the Retail Banking Division and is responsible for retention strategies, customer engagement, and portfolio stability.*

*It collaborates with Marketing, Digital Channels, and Risk Management teams to support customer relationship management.\]*

## Strategic Alignment 

*\[Explain how the business domain relates to the organization’s strategic priorities and why it is relevant at a strategic level.\]*

*\[Link the business area to strategic goals such as growth, efficiency, risk management, or customer experience.*

*Demonstrate why the domain is important without yet defining the problem.*

*Avoid describing current issues \- focus on strategic intent and direction.\]*

*\[**Example:** Customer retention is a key pillar of the organization’s strategy to increase customer lifetime value and ensure sustainable revenue growth.*

*The company’s strategic roadmap emphasizes proactive customer management and data-driven decision-making.\]*

## Current Situation Overview 

*\[Provide an objective, high-level description of how the relevant business processes currently operate.\]*

*\[Describe the current operating model, including:*

* *existing processes and workflows*  
* *how decisions are currently made*  
* *what data or tools are being used*

*Focus on observable facts without framing them as problems.*

*Avoid evaluative language (e.g., “inefficient”, “insufficient”) — that belongs to Section [Business Problem Statement](#business-problem-statement).\]*

*\[**Example**: Customer retention activities are currently based on periodic campaign analysis and manually defined segmentation rules.*

*Customer behavior is reviewed retrospectively using historical data extracts.*

*Engagement actions are triggered after predefined events such as inactivity or account changes.\]*

# Business Problem Statement 

*\[Formally define the business problem based on the previously described context, ensuring a clear and shared understanding of the issue.\]*

*\[Translate the contextual information into a clearly articulated business problem.*

*The problem should:*

* *describe what is not working or missing*  
* *be expressed in business terms*  
* *reflect impact on performance or strategic goals*

*This section introduces interpretation and evaluation, unlike Section [Business Context](#business-context).\]*

*\[**Example**: Despite established retention processes, the organization lacks the ability to proactively identify customers at risk of churn.*

*As a result, retention actions are primarily reactive and occur too late to influence customer decisions.\]*

## Problem Description 

*\[Provide a concise and precise definition of the core business problem.\]*

*\[Formulate the problem as a clear statement describing:*

* *the current limitation or gap*  
* *the affected business area*  
* *the missing capability or undesired condition*

*Avoid including impact or consequences \- focus strictly on the problem itself.\]*

*\[**Example**: The organization does not have a mechanism to identify customers at risk of churn before disengagement occurs.*

*Customer attrition is detected only after it has already taken place.\]*

## Business Impact 

*\[Explain how the identified problem affects business performance and why it matters.\]*

*\[Describe the tangible consequences of the problem in terms of:*

* *financial impact (revenue loss, cost increase)*  
* *operational inefficiencies*  
* *strategic or competitive disadvantages*

*Ensure the impact is directly linked to the problem defined in [Problem Description](#problem-description)\]*

*\[**Example**:  Customer attrition leads to a direct loss of recurring revenue and increases the cost of acquiring new customers to replace those lost.*

*Reactive retention efforts require additional operational resources without guaranteeing effectiveness.\]*

## Consequences of Inaction 

*\[Highlight the risks and long-term implications if the problem is not addressed.\]*

*\[Describe what is likely to happen if no action is taken, focusing on:*

* *worsening of current conditions*  
* *accumulation of negative effects over time*  
* *risks to strategic objectives*

*Differentiate from [Business Impact](#business-impact) by emphasizing future trajectory rather than current impact.\]*

*\[**Example**: If no changes are made, customer churn will continue to erode the customer base and reduce long-term profitability.*

*Over time, increasing acquisition costs and declining customer loyalty may weaken the organization’s market position.\]*

# Intended Business Outcome 

*\[Define the intended direction of improvement by outlining the value, future state, objectives, and success definition associated with resolving the business problem.\]*

*\[Provide a structured description of the desired outcome, ensuring a logical progression:*

* *why solving the problem is valuable (business value)*  
* *how the organization will operate differently (future state)*  
* *what objectives will be achieved (objectives)*  
* *how success will be understood (success definition)*

*This section bridges problem definition and solution design.\]*

*\[**Example**: By addressing the identified problem, the organization aims to improve customer retention effectiveness, enable proactive engagement, and strengthen long-term customer relationships.*

*The outcome includes both operational improvements and measurable business benefits aligned with strategic priorities.\]*

## Expected Business Value 

*\[Explain why solving the problem is valuable from a business perspective, focusing on the expected benefits and strategic impact.\]*

*\[Describe the expected business benefits resulting from addressing the problem.*

*Focus on:*

* *Financial impact (revenue, cost, efficiency)*  
* *Strategic impact (competitive position, customer value)*  
* *Operational improvements (process efficiency, decision quality)*

*Avoid describing how the solution will work \- focus only on why it matters.*

*Do not describe the future operating model in detail (this belongs to [Desired Future State](#desired-future-state)).\]*

*\[**Example**: Reducing customer churn is expected to increase recurring revenue and improve customer lifetime value.*

*Improved retention efficiency will lower acquisition costs required to replace lost customers.*

*The initiative supports long-term portfolio stability and strengthens competitive positioning.\]*

## Desired Future State 

*\[Describe how the organization will operate differently once the problem is addressed.\]*

*\[Define the future operational model enabled by solving the problem.*

*Focus on:*

* *New capabilities (e.g., predictive insights, automation)*  
* *Changes in processes or workflows*  
* *Improvements in decision-making*

*Do not repeat business value (already defined in [Expected Business Value](#expected-business-value)).*

*Describe how the system behaves, not why it is beneficial.\]*

*\[**Example:** The organization operates a proactive customer retention process where at-risk customers are identified in advance.*

*Business teams receive regular insights on churn risk and can initiate targeted retention actions.*

*Customer engagement decisions are supported by structured, data-driven signals rather than retrospective analysis.\]*

## Business Objectives 

*\[Clearly define what the organization aims to achieve by addressing the identified business problem, expressed in business terms and aligned with strategic goals.\]*

*\[Formulate a concise and explicit set of business objectives that directly respond to the problem statement and support the intended business outcome.*

*Objectives must:*

* *Be stated in business language (not technical or analytical terms)*  
* *Be specific enough to guide decision-making*  
* *Be aligned with strategic priorities defined in Section [Strategic Alignment](#strategic-alignment)*  
* *Reflect measurable direction, even if exact metrics are defined later*  
* *Avoid describing solutions (e.g., models, algorithms)*

*Each objective should answer:  “What do we want to achieve from a business perspective?”*

*If multiple objectives exist, prioritize them or distinguish between primary and secondary objectives.\]*

*\[**Example:*** 

*Primary Objective:*

*Reduce customer churn in the subscription segment by improving retention of high-value customers.*

*Secondary Objectives:*

* *Increase customer lifetime value through targeted retention actions*  
* *Improve understanding of key drivers behind customer attrition*  
* *Enable proactive engagement strategies for at-risk customers\]*

## Success Definition (Business-Level) 

*\[Define what constitutes a successful outcome of the project from a business perspective, establishing a clear and shared understanding of success before introducing technical metrics.\]*

*\[Describe how success will be evaluated by business stakeholders in qualitative or high-level quantitative terms.*

*This definition should:*

* *Reflect expected business impact (e.g., revenue, cost reduction, risk mitigation)*  
* *Be understandable to non-technical stakeholders*  
* *Precede and guide the definition of detailed success metrics in Task 1.3 Define Success Criteria*  
* *Clarify whether success is measured objectively (e.g., % improvement) or subjectively (e.g., “actionable insights”)*  
* *Identify decision-makers responsible for validating success if subjective criteria are used*

*Avoid specifying detailed model or technical metrics (these belong to Task 1.3 Define Success Criteria).*

*This section acts as a bridge between:*

* *Business Objectives (what we want)*  
* *Success Metrics (how we measure it technically)\]*

*\[**Example:** The project will be considered successful if:*

* *The organization is able to identify customers at high risk of churn early enough to take preventive action*  
* *Retention strategies informed by the project lead to a measurable improvement in customer retention rates*  
* *Business stakeholders confirm that the insights generated are actionable and support decision-making*

*Success validation will be performed jointly by the Head of Customer Retention and the Marketing Director.\]*
